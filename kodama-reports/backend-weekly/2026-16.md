## Notes
- **Canary files observed, not flagged:** `api/router.js`, `lib/pool.js`, `lib/supabase.js`, `lib/voice.js`, `vercel.json`, `package.json`, `routes/evaluate.js`. All present and in their expected shape.
- **Stable pattern — small, disciplined `lib/`.** Three helpers, each clearly scoped (Postgres pool, Supabase client, voice utilities). This is a long-standing stable pattern; no changes recommended.
- **Stable pattern — lazy-loading of heavy/risky routes.** `evaluate`, `assess`, `tts`, and `pronunciation-gpt` are imported via `lazyRoute()` so that one bad import cannot take down the whole single-function router. Continue to treat this guarantee as load-bearing when editing those four routes.
- **`.GOLD` / `.GOLD.2` snapshot files** are present in `routes/` (e.g., `assess.js.GOLD`, `attempt.js.GOLD.2`). They are not imported by `api/router.js` and therefore do not affect registration; they were excluded from every count above. Not an architectural concern this week, but worth a future decision about whether to move them out of `routes/` to keep the directory scan clean.
- **Admin gating** in `ROUTES` is consistent: all 10 cost-bearing routes are in `ADMIN_ONLY`; the four public-ish routes (`ping`, `health`, `admin-*` which use the same admin token, `attempt`, `migrate`, `update-attempt`, `user-recent`) are intentionally not in that set. No drift detected.
- **Headroom on the 400-line rule:** `convo-turn.js` at 363 lines is the closest to the threshold and has been trending upward across recent audits. Not a finding this week, but worth watching — if it crosses 400 next week, it will need a split-vs-keep call.

## Scope exclusions honored
`node_modules/`, `.vercel/`, and underscore-prefixed folders (e.g., `_agents-archive/`) were not scanned. `.GOLD*` snapshot files under `routes/` were excluded from line-count, import-count, and router checks.
