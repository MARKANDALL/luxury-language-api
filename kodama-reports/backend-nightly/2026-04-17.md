## Notes
- Canary files reviewed for context only, not flagged: `api/router.js`, `lib/pool.js`, `lib/supabase.js`, `lib/voice.js`, `vercel.json`, `package.json`, `routes/evaluate.js`. The router centralizes CORS, admin-token detection (`isAdminRequest`), and lazy-loads risky routes (`evaluate`, `assess`, `tts`, `pronunciation-gpt`) — which explains why per-route CORS is absent by design.
- Silent-catch hotspot is the OpenAI JSON-parse fallback pattern (convo-turn, convo-report, alt-meaning, runCoach). These are deliberate recoveries but they discard the raw GPT payload silently, which will frustrate post-hoc debugging of model drift. Consider a single shared `safeParseJson(raw, context)` helper that `console.warn`s before repair.
- Auth-gate gap on `voice-clone` / `voice-mirror` / `assess` is the highest-signal finding in this scan — three paid external APIs (ElevenLabs, ElevenLabs, Azure Speech) currently reachable without `ADMIN_TOKEN`. Worth prioritizing above the silent-catch cleanups.
- Helper-level catches in `routes/realtime-webrtc-session.js:190` and `routes/tts/speak.js:98` return structured error objects to their callers (which are handlers with proper top-level logging) — not flagged.
- Scan/utility catches in `scripts/hygiene-report.backend.mjs` (186, 244, 250) are documented intentional skips in a filesystem-walk loop — not flagged.
